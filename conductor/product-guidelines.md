# Product Guidelines

## Communication & Tone
- **Professional & Clinical:** Use direct, precise, and objective language for all system messages, notifications, and labels. Avoid ambiguity to ensure medical accuracy and user confidence.

## Visual Design Principles
- **Clarity First:** Information architecture and typography must be optimized for fast, accurate reading of complex medical data. Prioritize legibility over aesthetics.
- **High Contrast:** Ensure all UI elements meet high contrast standards (WCAG 2.1 AA) to maintain readability across diverse environments (clinics, labs, home use) and for all patient demographics.

## Interaction & Motion
- **Functional & Subtle:** Utilize Framer Motion for smooth, logical transitions between application states, providing visual cues without causing distraction.
- **Snappy & Responsive:** Ensure immediate visual feedback for all user actions, particularly clinical data entry, to confirm system registration of input.
- **Standard & Familiar:** Leverage established Radix UI and shadcn/ui interaction patterns to minimize the learning curve for both medical staff and patients.

## Accessibility
- **WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance:** Adhere to universal accessibility standards for both the administrative and patient-facing portals.
- **Screen Reader Optimized:** Maintain semantic HTML and ARIA roles to ensure the application is fully usable for vision-impaired patients and staff.
- **Keyboard Navigable:** Enable full system control via keyboard to increase data entry efficiency for clinical staff and support users with motor impairments.

## Platform Experience
- **Full Feature Parity:** The application must offer a complete, non-compromised experience on tablets and desktop, enabling medical staff to perform all tasks across devices.
- **Optimized Data Entry:** Implement touch-friendly controls and custom input patterns specifically designed for efficient clinical data entry on mobile and tablet devices.
